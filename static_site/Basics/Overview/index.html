Overview
Introduction to T-Spin Techniques
This guide will attempt to explain T-Spin setups, from the fundamentals. There have been many guides out there and tons of helpful documentaion on harddrop.com and tetris.wiki, but despite the immense usefullness of these sites for getting to know how to do setups and gameplay techniques, I can't seem to find any articles that attempts to explain everything from the basics.
My goal is to compile a tightly structured list of T-Spin setups and explain how and each of every single one of them is designed, and that the reader should leave this guide knowing how they could've designed these setups themselves. And ultimately, I want you to feel confident enough to make a T-Spin setup of your own. Notice I will laser focus on the core empty shapes that define T-Spin setups, not the outer stacking with actual specific tetrominos used to build them.
Knowing how to design T-Spins setups benefits one's soul greatly for the following reasons:
  1. It's fun
  2. T-Spin setups are aesthetically beautiful
  3. Knowing how these setups are built really helps you be more aware of efficient building structures in real battles
So without further ado.... Here's the guide
What Are T-Spins?
T-Spins are advanced techniques where a T-piece is rotated into a cavity that cannot be entered through simple horizontal movement. They provide bonus points and are essential for competitive play.
Types of T-Spins
There are several types of T-Spins, each with different setups and applications:
  • T-Spin Single: Clears 1 line with T-Spin bonus
  • T-Spin Double: Clears 2 lines with T-Spin bonus
  • T-Spin Triple: Clears 3 lines with T-Spin bonus
